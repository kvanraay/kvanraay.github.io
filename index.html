Katrina van Raay

I am a PhD student in the Kerr Lab at the University of Washington. I have used bacteriophages (viruses that infect bacteria) to study evolution, with a particular interest in how bacteriophage evolutionary trajectories are shaped by antagonistic interactions with their bacterial hosts.

I am strongly committed to outreach and increasing diversity in STEM. Thus far I have done work volunteering in an elementary school, high school, and a prison in addition to teaching at the undergraduate level.
